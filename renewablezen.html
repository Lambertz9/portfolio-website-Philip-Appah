RenewableZen

Introduction

RenewableZen is my first project as a UX Designer. It is a responsive web app that was delivered in fulfilment of certification in UX Immersion by CareerFoundry. I was motivated to embark on this project because it will offer energy solutions to a sizeable proportion of the society.

My Role

As a UX designer, my role was to apply a user-centred process to identify the problems, establish a hypothesis, conduct research, and come up with a design solution.

Tools

  • Survey: Survey Monkey
  • Sitemap: Flowmapp
  • A/B Test: Usability Hub
  • Wireframe: Balsamiq
  • Prototype: Adobe XD

The Problem

It is complicated for individuals to find desired renewable energy experts in the field who will provide for them the needed amount of information to satisfy their preferential needs. There are insufficient direct and less bureaucratic mediums to connect with energy experts.

Hypothesis

I believe that creating an app where users can conveniently search for an energy expert that is available upon request will go a long way to satisfying the needs of users.

06 Usability Testing

I began the exercise by formulating my recruitment documents and consent forms. This was later followed by designing a Usability Test Plan and the Test Script.  Test goals and objectives were formulated and included in the usability test plan. The usability test goals are as follows: Examine if users are able to complete tasks, check users learnability, and to check convenient levels and durations of tasks completion.

Usability Testing Results

Affinity Mapping was used to isolate the results and to identify patterns and hierarchies. On the Test Report, 5 of the errors were selected and prioritised according to their level of severity based on the Jacob Nelsons error scale (See graphic below). The full test report and Rainbow Spread Sheet can be accessed here

Prototype Review

Based on findings from the Usability Test exercise, the prioritised issues were fixed. The before and after screens below shows a rectification made based on one of the usability issues identified (users weren’t triggered by the “live chat” CTA on the expert’s screen), I changed the button to “book live chat”.

07 User Interface (UI)

After careful readjustments of my prototype based on feedback from colleagues, I was ready to apply finishing touches (digital makeup) to my screens. I applied relevant UI principles such as the Gestalt principles and guidelines for accessibility. I also developed a Design Language System to guide the design of the product across multiple platforms and devices. Reflection

The project being my first project ever as a UX Designer means a great deal to me especially how I was able to personalise the initial brief which was a general expert seeking app into an app to seek renewable energy experts. I was able to learn and adapt to the rudiments of UX Design and basic application of UI principles. I felt and still feel intrigued about how I was able to put together a design that isn’t only aesthetically pleasing but also convenient for users to execute defined tasks. I look forward to my next project, I am keen on improving on my design skills a bit a time.
